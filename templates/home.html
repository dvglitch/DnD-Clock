⚔️ How Timer-Based Combat Works

How It Works

Actions work like turn-based combat, but timers replace initiative order. Each combatant has a countdown — when your timer hits 0, you may take your action regardless of whose "turn" it is. After acting, reset your timer to start the cooldown again. Initiative still matters: it determines how long each player's cooldown is, so higher initiative means you act more frequently.

Movement & Bonus Actions

Movement and bonus actions are not tracked by the timer. You may use your movement and bonus action freely once per action cycle. They replenish after you take your next action.

Combos

If two or more players hit 0 at the same time, they may combine their actions for a stat bonus at the DM's discretion. Coordinate with your party to set up devastating joint strikes!

Getting Started

The DM sets up combatants on the Control Panel and projects the Display on a shared screen. Players scan the QR code to get their own Remote. Hit start and begin fighting!

The goal is to reduce downtime between turns and keep every player engaged throughout combat. Adjust any of these rules to fit your campaign — this is your tool, make it work for you.
